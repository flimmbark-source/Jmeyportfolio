CASE STUDY

The Intelligent Scouting Archive

A personal project applying mixed-methods research to solve a real-world data challenge in sports analytics.

The Situation: A Hidden Talent Pool

While American collegiate sports are data-rich, European university basketball represents a significant, yet underserved, talent pool. Player information is often ephemeral, scattered across temporary tournament sites that are difficult to track. This "data obscurity" creates a significant challenge for scouts who rely on timely and accurate information.

My Action & Methodology

1. Qualitative Research: Understanding the User

As a curious, people-first researcher, my first step was to understand the end-user. I conducted in-depth interviews with several basketball scouts to map their daily workflows and pinpoint their primary frustrations. This qualitative feedback was clear: the most time-consuming part of their job was not analysis, but the manual, repetitive task of basic data collection. This finding became the cornerstone of the project.

2. Quantitative Framework: Designing for Insight

With a clear user need, I designed the system's data architecture. My goal was to create a database that could not only store information but also facilitate statistical analysis (e.g., correlation analysis between player stats and team performance). I structured the data schema to ensure that all research findings could be translated into actionable, data-driven recommendations for scouts.

3. Technical Implementation: Building the Solution

Leveraging my technical skills, I built a functional prototype. I developed the front-end using HTML, Tailwind CSS, and JavaScript, focusing on a clean and intuitive user interface. For the back-end, I implemented a real-time database using Google Firebase. The core of the application is an AI powered by the Google Gemini API, for which I engineered a series of highly specific prompts to handle the nuanced task of finding and parsing data from niche sports websites.

The Result: An Intelligent Assistant

The result is a powerful web application that directly addresses the problems identified in my initial research. The app successfully:

  • ✓ Discovers and archives players from obscure sources.
  • ✓ Automatically enriches profiles with stats, summaries, and sources.
  • ✓ Continuously updates itself to ensure data freshness.
  • ✓ Provides a clean, user-friendly interface for scouts.
